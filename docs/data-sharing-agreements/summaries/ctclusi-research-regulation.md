## What this is

The catalog classifies this document as **research-code**. Covered parties: Individuals, corporations, agencies, and public or private institutions seeking to conduct studies, surveys, or research projects on the Tribes' lands when the work was not specifically requested or contracted for by the Tribes; Tribal employees also have a records-nondisclosure duty. Covered data or activity: Study, survey, and research designs, funding and personnel information, source information, results and products, intended publications, Tribal files and records, and interim, progress, and final reports concerning projects on the Tribes' lands.

| `ctclusi-research-regulation:c001` | `consent-fpic` | `must-shall` | Any individual, corporation, agency, or public or private institution wishing to undertake a study, survey, or research project on the Tribes' lands must first obtain a permit approved by the Tribal Council or its designate. | Applies when the project was not specifically requested or contracted for by the Tribes. | § 1-10-3 (PDF p. 1) |

| `ctclusi-research-regulation:c004` | `review-board` | `must-shall` | Before a permit may issue, the applicant must provide for approval the project's purpose and profit status, funding source and amount, methodology, participants, dates, information sources, intended final product and publication plan, intended use of results, and steps protecting Tribal and member rights. | Approval is by the Tribal Council chair or designate; a performance bond is additionally required only when the Tribes deem it appropriate. | § 1-10-5(a)-(j) (PDF pp. 1-2) |
| `ctclusi-research-regulation:c005` | `enforcement-remedies` | `may-permissive` | The Tribes may cancel a permit if the project is or has been conducted contrary to the approved study design or the agreement supporting the permit. | Cancellation is conditioned on an apparent deviation from the approved design or underlying agreement. | § 1-10-6 (PDF p. 2) |
| `ctclusi-research-regulation:c006` | `review-board` | `may-permissive` | The Tribes may refuse a permit when they determine that there is insufficient time for appropriate Tribal review, evaluation, supervision, or participation. | Applies when the Tribes deem review time insufficient. | § 1-10-7 (PDF p. 2) |
| `ctclusi-research-regulation:c007` | `benefit-sharing` | `may-permissive` | The Tribes may charge a permit fee when the project is conducted for profit or its results or publication are intended or likely to financially benefit the researcher or research organization. | Limited to for-profit projects or projects whose results or publication are intended or likely to produce financial benefit. | § 1-10-8 (PDF p. 3) |
| `ctclusi-research-regulation:c008` | `data-return-destruction` | `must-shall` | The researcher must furnish the Tribal Chair's office, without charge, at least one copy of every interim or progress report and the final report resulting from the project. | Applies to reports resulting from a study, survey, or research project governed by the chapter. | § 1-10-10 (PDF p. 3) |
| `ctclusi-research-regulation:c009` | `redisclosure-limits` | `must-shall` | Tribal employees must not provide information from Tribal files or records to a person or organization conducting research that is not authorized under the chapter. | The nondisclosure duty applies when the recipient's research is not authorized under Chapter 1-10; violation exposes the employee to censure or dismissal. | § 1-10-11 (PDF p. 3) |
| `ctclusi-research-regulation:c010` | `enforcement-remedies` | `must-shall` | A person attempting research that was neither specifically requested or contracted for by the Tribes nor permitted under the chapter is subject to available civil remedies under Tribal and Oregon law. | Applies only to research outside both stated authorization paths. | § 1-10-12 (PDF p. 3) |

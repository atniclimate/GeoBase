## What it is

THRO Overview: Indigenous Data Sovereignty is cataloged as a `presentation` issued by National Institutes of Health Tribal Health Research Office. It covers: NIH-funded researchers and studies, Tribal Nations and communities, individual participants, Tribal review bodies, and data stewards discussed in the presentation's proposed Indigenous Data Sovereignty policy takeaways; the slides do not establish a final NIH policy.

The cataloged data scope is: Data and samples collected from or about Tribal populations; Tribal attributions and inferences; human-subject, biological, genetic, genomic, environmental, cultural, and Indigenous Knowledge materials; secondary analyses, publications, transfer, reuse, and disposal.

## TSDF relationship

**Outcome:** `partially-mapped`. **Closest tiers:** `T2`, `T3`.

Tribal approval before collection and reuse, opt-in access, restricted secondary analysis, and repatriation resemble negotiated T2 and sovereign T3 controls. Collective consent, Tribal IRB designation, benefit-sharing, cultural guardianship, re-consent, inference-based scope, and individual-versus-collective disposal rules are not fully represented by TSDF tiers.

This mapping is descriptive only. Anything the catalog marks `not-represented`, `not-applicable`, `needs-human-review`, or `in-tension` must not be inferred from a TSDF tier.

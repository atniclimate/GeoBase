## What it is

78 IAM 2: Data Governance is cataloged as a `agency-policy` issued by Bureau of Indian Affairs. It covers: All programs and offices under Bureau of Indian Affairs authority, including BIA leadership, deputy bureau directors, the associate chief data officer, data stewards, data asset custodians and producers, system owners, and employees, only within each role's assigned responsibilities.

The cataloged data scope is: All BIA data assets collected, created, or acquired; metadata, information-system schemas and dictionaries, public and nonpublic data, controlled and personally identifiable information, geospatial data, open-government data assets, and data supplied to AI systems.

## TSDF relationship

**Outcome:** `in-tension`. **Closest tiers:** `T0`, `T3`.

The directive's nonpublic-data and role-based controls partially resemble restricted handling, but its statutory open-data default applies unless law or regulation restricts release. TSDF instead treats unclassified data as T3 and can preserve sovereign restrictions not expressed solely as federal law or regulation; the directive contains no TSDF-tier or T3-egress guarantee.

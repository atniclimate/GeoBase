# Enforcement, remedies, jurisdiction, and immunity boundaries

This draft searches the entire merged catalog (64 records / 359 claims) and restates 36 claims across 19 records for this theme. It does not average different Nations' requirements or treat templates, guidance, drafts, scholarship, or unresolved instruments as enacted law.

## Range across instruments

- The strongest cataloged enacted remedies include Siletz Tribal Court authority for injunctions, preservation and return of data, bond forfeiture, restitution, civil penalties, and other relief; Grand Ronde escalating civil fines and loss of record-access rights; and unauthorized-research exposure to available Tribal civil or criminal remedies in Nez Perce and CTCLUSI instruments (`siletz-research-ordinance:c013`; `grand-ronde-foi-ordinance:c009`; `nez-perce-2024-revised-research-permit-regulations-application:c007`; `ctclusi-research-regulation:c010`).
- Permit enforcement also includes conditional cancellation, denial, revocation, suspension, corrective statements, or false-information consequences. Each trigger and cure period remains claim-specific (`ctclusi-research-regulation:c005`; `coeur-dalene-research-permit-application:c005`; `nez-perce-2024-revised-research-permit-regulations-application:c009`; `nez-perce-2024-revised-research-permit-regulations-application:c014`; `nez-perce-2024-revised-research-permit-regulations-application:c020`; `colville-research-permit-application:c003`; `colville-research-permit-application:c008`; `colville-chapter-6-6-research-regulation:c004`; `nez-perce-revised-code-title-3:c002`).
- Jurisdiction ranges from Siletz deemed consent to Tribal legislative, regulatory, and adjudicatory jurisdiction for covered research, to permit-template assurances requiring compliance with Tribal law, to appeal-record transmission duties and a narrowly conditioned draft off-reservation pathway (`siletz-research-ordinance:c001`; `coeur-dalene-research-permit-application:c002`; `coeur-dalene-research-permit-application:c003`; `nez-perce-revised-code-title-3:c017`; `yakama-water-code:c009`; `inst-003:c014`).
- The catalog does not support inferring a waiver of Tribal sovereign immunity. Its express enforcement-boundary claim instead concerns EO 13175: the order creates no judicially enforceable right or benefit and does not diminish federal trust responsibilities (`r3-005:c011`). This is a catalog boundary, not a statement that no immunity clause exists elsewhere.

## Separate government-to-government control plane

RCW 43.376 duties operate separately from artifact classification: agency collaboration and consultation processes, designated liaisons, communication and training, annual activity reports and meetings, and convening duties govern institutional relationships and process (`r3-002:c001`; `r3-002:c002`; `r3-002:c003`; `r3-002:c004`; `r3-002:c005`; `r3-002:c006`; `r3-002:c007`; `r3-002:c008`). They are not substitutes for TSDF artifact controls.

## TSDF relationship

Jurisdiction, forum, remedies, cure periods, penalties, and enforceability boundaries are legal-process controls, not artifact classifications. A TSDF tier cannot supply them (`siletz-research-ordinance:c001`; `siletz-research-ordinance:c013`; `r3-005:c011`).

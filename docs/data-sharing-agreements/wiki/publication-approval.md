# Publication approval and pre-publication review

This draft searches the entire merged catalog (64 records / 359 claims) and restates 14 claims across 9 records for this theme. It does not average different Nations' requirements or treat templates, guidance, drafts, scholarship, or unresolved instruments as enacted law.

## Range across instruments

- The cataloged range includes an express written-permission condition for publication of covered Siletz minor projects, an agreement requirement addressing Nez Perce prepublication review, final authorization, and publication parameters, and an NWIC IRB power to approve, modify, or disapprove covered outputs (`siletz-research-ordinance:c005`; `nez-perce-2024-revised-research-permit-regulations-application:c017`; `nwic-irb-policy-806:c008`).
- Other enacted provisions are narrower: CTCLUSI and Colville require an opportunity to review and comment and inclusion of official comments, but the catalog conditions expressly do not restate those provisions as an unqualified publication veto (`ctclusi-research-regulation:c003`; `colville-chapter-6-6-research-regulation:c002`).
- Permissive and notice-based controls must remain distinct: Siletz may prevent specified unauthorized, insensitive, misrepresentative, stereotyping, or harmful publication; the Coeur d'Alene template permits sponsor review 30 days before publication or presentation and separately requires presentation notice (`siletz-research-ordinance:c010`; `coeur-dalene-research-permit-application:c006`; `coeur-dalene-research-permit-application:c008`).
- Nonbinding and draft materials add conditional processes rather than confirmed Nation requirements: NIH recommends discussing Tribal preapproval processes, NWTEC imposes provider- and product-specific institutional review paths, and the draft THRO presentation describes formal approval for Tribe-specific inference (`bl-032:c006`; `d2bl-001:c019`; `d2bl-001:c020`; `d2bl-001:c021`; `d2bl-001:c023`; `inst-003:c016`).

## TSDF relationship

Prepublication review, comment, approval, notice, and final-authorization controls are process rights that a tier label does not encode. They must remain agreement- or policy-specific (`ctclusi-research-regulation:c003`; `siletz-research-ordinance:c005`; `nez-perce-2024-revised-research-permit-regulations-application:c017`).

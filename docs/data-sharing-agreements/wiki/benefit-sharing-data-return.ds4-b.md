# Benefit sharing and data return or destruction

This draft searches the entire merged catalog (64 records / 359 claims) and restates 25 claims across 14 records for this theme. It does not average different Nations' requirements or treat templates, guidance, drafts, scholarship, or unresolved instruments as enacted law.

## Range across instruments

- Cataloged benefit mechanisms range from local-work preference and a profit-contingent Tribal royalty to permit fees, performance bonds, or consultant-cost contributions conditioned on profit or review needs (`nez-perce-2024-revised-research-permit-regulations-application:c011`; `nez-perce-2024-revised-research-permit-regulations-application:c018`; `ctclusi-research-regulation:c007`; `siletz-research-ordinance:c006`; `colville-research-permit-application:c005`; `colville-chapter-6-6-research-regulation:c006`).
- Reciprocity and authorship appear with weaker legal force: the Coeur d'Alene template states expectations of respect, reciprocity, relationship, and benefit; NWTEC recommends qualifying co-authorship invitations and acknowledgement; draft THRO material recommends plain-language individual and collective benefit terms (`coeur-dalene-research-permit-application:c001`; `d2bl-001:c022`; `inst-003:c004`).
- Return duties range from providing final or interim products to returning all information, data, raw materials, and specimens after withdrawal of approval (`ctclusi-research-regulation:c008`; `coeur-dalene-research-permit-application:c007`; `nez-perce-2024-revised-research-permit-regulations-application:c006`; `colville-research-permit-application:c004`; `colville-chapter-6-6-research-regulation:c007`; `siletz-research-ordinance:c011`).
- Destruction and continuing control are more conditional: NWTEC ties retention, return, and destruction to provider agreements and specified end-of-project follow-up; its local-copy rule requires secure shredding after analysis. Separate guidance describes continuing opt-out/removal, while draft THRO material preserves individual consent as a limit on Tribal disposal or repatriation choices (`d2bl-001:c007`; `d2bl-001:c011`; `inst-002:c004`; `inst-003:c009`; `inst-003:c017`).
- Other return records have narrower administrative scopes—including record-management policy, consultation minutes, permit harvest reports, and website-log destruction—and must not be generalized into research-data return rules (`grand-ronde-foi-ordinance:c001`; `klamath-special-use-permit:c007`; `nez-perce-revised-code-title-3:c007`; `id-005:c005`).

## TSDF relationship

TSDF tiers can restrict handling or egress, but they do not encode royalties, local hiring, authorship, report delivery, agreement-specific retention, end-of-project destruction, continuing opt-out, or individual-consent conflicts (`nez-perce-2024-revised-research-permit-regulations-application:c018`; `d2bl-001:c011`; `inst-002:c004`; `inst-003:c009`).

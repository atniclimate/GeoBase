## Failures

### BLOCKING — `yakama-water-code:c002`: modal upgraded from "may" to `must-shall`

- **File:** `catalog/catalog.jsonl`, record `yakama-water-code`
  (`corpus/yakama-nation/yakama-water-code/v1-d1634cc3.pdf`).
- **Catalog says:** "The Water Code Director and staff **must** collect and
  maintain statistical records concerning permit applications, issued
  permits, and other administrative activities under the Code."
  (`modal: must-shall`, cite Section 60.05.05(h), code p. 11 / PDF p. 11,
  conditions "Applies as an administrative duty under the Code.")
- **Bytes say (code p. 10, chapeau of 60.05.05):** "60.05.05:
  ADMINISTRATIVE DUTIES. The Director and, under his or her direction, the
  Water Code staff **may**: … (h) Collect and maintain statistical records
  concerning permits applied for and issued, and other administrative
  activities taken under this Code;"
- **Why blocking:** the operative verb governing item (h) is permissive
  "may", not "shall". Recording it as `must-shall` overstates the
  obligation strength of an enacted Yakama Nation instrument — precisely
  the charter §8 violation class ("a 'must' that loses its qualifier is as
  wrong as a 'must' that becomes 'should'", and the converse). The section
  heading "ADMINISTRATIVE DUTIES" is in tension with the permissive
  chapeau, but a faithful record must carry the enacted text's modal (or
  flag the heading/chapeau tension for human review), not resolve it
  upward silently.
- **Remediation:** director `correction` event against
  `ev-ds3-c-parse009`'s catalog record (same pattern as
  `ev-director-0083`) recording the corrected reading
  (`may-permissive`, or `needs-human-review` citing the duty-heading
  tension), then re-audit of this record.
- **Defect-class sweep (whole document, beyond the sample):** the other
  ten Yakama claims were checked against their cited sections; all other
  `must-shall` claims rest on genuine "shall" text (60.05.11, 60.09.15(a),
  60.09.25(a), 60.09.27(b), 60.13.01/03, 60.21.07(a), 60.29.05(a)) and
  c001 correctly records "may" as `may-permissive`. The failure is
  isolated to c002.

## DS-4 theme patterns

1. **Tribal authorization is an independent control plane.** The NWTEC handbook and NIH draft takeaways require or recommend written Tribal permission, Tribal review-body selection, approval before collection or reuse, and consent for Tribe-specific inference. These controls do not reduce to a T2/T3 assignment.
2. **Purpose, role, and recipient restrictions are more granular than tiers.** NWTEC distinguishes Tribal employees, outside entities, contractors, data providers, authors, media, and system-specific users; the Kalispel–Commerce agreement limits sharing to two parties, a narrow anti-duplication purpose, listed fields, and necessity. DS-4 should preserve these predicates rather than treating all authorized access as interchangeable.
3. **Publication and secondary analysis remain materially underrepresented.** Prior review, provider approval, Tribal consent for publication or Tribe-specific inference, re-consent, authorship, acknowledgement, and repatriation appear repeatedly. TSDF access tiers alone do not encode these continuing controls.
4. **Small-number and inference risks create contextual disclosure boundaries.** NWTEC uses general and provider-specific suppression rules and prohibits reconstruction of suppressed cells; the NIH presentation triggers Tribal approval based on potential inference or impact even when data are not explicitly labeled Tribal. A static artifact tier does not capture those analytic-context tests.
5. **Open-data defaults can conflict with precautionary sovereign defaults.** BIA 78 IAM 2 makes covered federal assets open by default unless law or regulation restricts them, while TSDF defaults unclassified material to T3 and can preserve sovereign restrictions that are not reducible to a federal legal exception. This is recorded as `in-tension`, not silently mapped.
6. **Request and agreement processes matter even without a full substantive instrument.** Swinomish recommends a structured research-request channel for sensitive archive materials; the BJA presentation treats government-to-government input, Tribal ownership, and bilateral exhibits as core agreement components. DS-4 should represent procedural gates and bilateral amendments alongside artifact classification.
7. **Index pages and public availability are not substantive permission.** Four constitution/code landing records, the BIA consultation page, and the NNI abstract yield honest `none_reason` results. Linked titles, purpose captions, or a public consultation do not establish the linked instrument's obligations or permission for downstream use.
